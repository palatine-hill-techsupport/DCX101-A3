About This Project

Created as part of Assignment 3 for DCX101 - Design Context by Bille Blue @ Torrens University Australia.

With this project, I aim to bring the design language and revolutionary spirit of the French Revolution into the present, filtered through the lens of UX and web design. My goal is to create an interactive tool that visually represents global freedom scores in a meaningful way. If it sparks even the smallest thought, conversation, or action, I’ll consider it a success.

Knowledge is power. This project is designed to be an engaging way to explore global freedom data while also providing access to local and international resources for those who seek change.

Data Source:
The data in this project is sourced from Freedom House:
“Freedom House rates people’s access to political rights and civil liberties in 210 countries and territories through its annual Freedom in the World report. Individual freedoms—ranging from the right to vote to freedom of expression and equality before the law—can be affected by state or non-state actors.”

Important Note:
This dataset was captured in January 2025 and is intended for project display purposes only. It does not represent real-time data. This also applies to the global and local resources listed.
